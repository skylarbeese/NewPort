Overview

Everyone uses email to communicate and send information. Many people still use it as their primary means of communication. This project will consist of designing a new email site that is visually sleek and modern in its design as well as making it easier to use and find the necessary tools.

My Role

I conducted user research to learn the user's habits and better understand their needs. I then created a persona based on my findings and also conducted a competitive analysis of different email platforms. I took note of what works and what does not on the different sites and implement that in my design. After that, I will take my findings and design a new UI for an email platform.

The main problem and goal

Users are busy, they need to be able to use an email the platform quickly and efficiently to get their tasks completed in a timely the manner by sending information and communicating to whoever they need to.

Having a visually modern design and the most used tools, whether that is finding their sent, spam, or most important emails.

Requirements

  • Make the most used functions the most prominent
  • Create a slide out menu for all the tools
  • Make sure the search bar is clear and easy to find
  • Make things look sleek and modern

Discovery and Research

I needed to get to know the users and their main needs for the platform. That also included getting to know their habits and uses for their email. Most users tend to check their email multiple times a day. They receive and send all kinds of mail from different sources either personal, professional for their work or school and other sources. Some of the tools that they use most are finding their sent mail, and mail marked as important.

Competitive analysis

After this, I also felt that it was important to research other email sites out there to get a feel of how they work and how they are designed. I wanted to take note of the aspects that I thought worked and those that did not. Many of them had the same fundamental layout. The search bar is at the top right, the toolbar on the left side. I wanted to keep the same general layout so that it would make it easier for new users coming over from other websites to use this new layout. I also found that the UI for that website did not look modern and felt that it could be updated.

Personas

User personas were constructed based on the findings. This allowed target users to be identified and their main needs and habits to be addressed.

Ideation

From the research, I found that most of the email platforms had the same basic layout. I wanted to use this so when I started wireframing for the new website I kept the same basic layout and most of the aspects of the site, search bar, slide-out menu bar, mail lists in the same places. I also wanted to create a more modern look and feel to the new design. I laid out the most important aspects first on the menu. I wanted the compose button to be prominent and able to be seen even when the menu was closed.

Solution

The original need was to create a new email page that was easier to use were the most important aspects were prominent and could be found easily. All this needed to be done well to give the new email platform a more modern look and feel that other sites out there did not have. This is the final mockup of the website and what the finished design will look like.

The final stages were prototyping and testing the new design. The working prototype can be viewed here. The feedback I got from the user test will go into revisions for the final site.

Prototyping and User Testing

The prototype was created in Abode XD. For the final testing, I had my users go through a handful of tasks to ensure that the design and functionality of the site would be the best possible. Using the previous user persona created I ask the testers to complete the following tasks and assess how they performed. The first is to compose a new message and send it. Another was asking them now to find the mail they save as important. I finished by getting their feedback on the design.

It was easy to navigate the site and the users were able to complete the first assessment rather easily. However, the wording on the menu to find the saved and important messages were confusing to the users.

Conclusion

I believe that this application has a lot of great potential for users. I would like to continue refining the app to make it more user friendly and dive deeper into how it can solve users' problems.
