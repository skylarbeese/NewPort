Overview

Everyone uses email to communicate and send information. Many people still use it as their primary means of communication. This project will consist of designing a new email site that is visually sleek and modern in its design as well as making it easier to use and find the necessary tools.

My Role

I will be conducting user research to learn the users habits and better understand their needs. I will be creating a persona based on my findings. I will also be looking at several different platforms and reviewing their designs. I will be taking note of what works and what does not and implement that in my design. I will be taking the information that I have gathered to the design process as I start to construct wireframes and later final mockups.

The main problem and goal

Users are busy and it is important for them to be able to use an email platform quickly and efficiently to get their tasks completed in a timely manner by sending information and communicating to whoever they need to.

Having a visually modern design and the most used tools, whether that is finding their sent, spam, or most important emails.

Reqiuremants for this project

  • Make the most used functions the post prominent
  • Create a slide out menu for all the tools
  • Make sure the search bar is clear and easy to find
  • Make things look sleek and modern

Discovery and Research

Having the goal of the project in mind we need to get to know the users and their main needs for the platform. That will also include getting to know their habits and uses for their email. Most users tend to check their email multiple times a day. They receive and send all kinds of mail from different sources either personal, professional for their work or school and other sources. Some of the tools that they use most are finding their sent mail, and mail marked as important.

Checking out the competition

After this I also felt that it was important to research other email sites out there to get a feel of how they work and there designed. I wanted to take note of the aspects that I thought worked and those that did not. Many of them had the same fundamental lay out. The search bar at the top right, the tool bar at the left side. I wanted to keep the same general layout so that it would make it easier for new users coming over from other websites to use this new layout. I also found that the UI for that website did not look modern and felt that it could be updated.

Personas

User personas were constructed based on the findings. This will allow target users to be indentified and their main needs and habits to be addressed.

Ideation

From the research I found that most of the email platforms had the same basic layout. I wanted to use this so when I started wireframing for the new website I kept the same basic layout and most of the aspects of the site, searchbar, slide out menu bar, mail lists in the same places. I also wanted to create a more modern look and feel to the new design. I layed out the most important aspects first on the menu. I wanted the compose button to be prominent and able to be seen even when the menu was closed.

Solution

The original need was to create a new email page that was easier to use where the most important aspects were prominent and could be found easily. All this needed to be done well to give the new email platform a more modern look and feel that other sites out there did not have. This is the final mockup of the website and what the finished design will look like.

The final stage is prototyping and testing the new design. The working prototype can be viewed here. The feedback from the user test can go into reviewing the final site. Revisions may need to be done to ensure the design will create the best experience for the user as possible.

Prototyping and User Testing

The prototype was created in Abode XD. For the final testing I had my users go through a handful of tasks to ensure that the design and functionality of the site would be the best possible. Using the previous user persona created I ask the testers to complete the following tasks and assess how they performed. The first being to compose a new message and send it. Another was asking them now to find the mail they save as important. I finished by getting their feedback on the design.

It was easy to navigate the site and the users were able to complete the first assessment rather easily. However the wording on the menu to find the saved and important messages was confusing to the testers.
