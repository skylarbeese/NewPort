Overview

Everyone uses email to communicate and send information. Many people still use it as their primary means of communication. This project will consist of designing a new email site that is visually sleek and modern in its design as well as making it easier to use and find the necessary tools.

My Role

I conducted user research to learn the user's habits and better understand their needs. I then created a persona based on my findings and also conducted a competitive analysis of different email platforms. I took note of what works and what does not on the different sites and implement that in my design. After that, I will take my findings and design a new UI for an email platform.

Understand

The main problem and goal

Users are busy, they need to be able to use an email the platform quickly and efficiently to get their tasks completed in a timely the manner by sending information and communicating to whoever they need to.

Having a visually modern design and the most used tools, whether that is finding their sent, spam, or most important emails.

Requirements

  • Make the most used functions the most prominent
  • Create a slide out menu for all the tools
  • Make sure the search bar is clear and easy to find
  • Make things look sleek and modern

Research

Discovery and Research

I needed to get to know the users and their main needs for the platform. That also included getting to know their habits and uses for their email. Most users tend to check their email multiple times a day. They receive and send all kinds of mail from different sources either personal, professional for their work or school and other sources. Some of the tools that they use most are finding their sent mail, and mail marked as important.

After getting more information about the target demographic I was able to create user personas based on my findings.

Design

Site Map

After the research on the users is done I started to take a look at the current webpage and began the first stages of the redesign. The sitemap helped view the content, flow of the pages, and the information that is laid out on each page.

Wireframes

After the site mapping is finished I start to layout the information on the pages creating wireframes to give us the beginning stages of what the finished site will look like.

The large majority of the people coming to the site will most likely be looking at it on their phones. I wanted to make sure the website will work for mobile devices as well.

Final UI

The color and images are incorporated after the final wireframes are created. The finished mockups will be what the new rendition of the site will look like. I wanted to ultimately make the information on the site easier for people to understand, and make the most important features that people would need for applying to the program be more prominent which will entice more people to enter the program.

Prototype

Prototyping and User Testing

The final stage of the redesigned website was to create and test the prototype of the new layout. I tested 3 different areas of the site to make sure future students would be able to find them. The 5 test users were high school to college age. They were asked to complete a handful of tasks on the website which included finding the advising and scholarships pages. They also were asked to find more information about the major itself. Our testing concluded I still had some redesigning and adjustments to make with some of the pages. Users were having a hard time finding the advising page. They were trying to find the advisers through the faculty and staff page. We fixed this problem by adding a link to the advising page on the faculty and staff page.
