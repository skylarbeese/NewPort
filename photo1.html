Overview

A local freelance photographer and videographer wanted to get more traffic to their business and have a place to show their work online. They needed a website to serve as their place to show and brand their work, and their business, as well as provide a way for clients to contact them. This project consisted of designing a functional web page for a small business that meets the needs of the client. I also designed a logo that they might use on their website, business cards, and products that they might like to sell in the future.

My Role

I was the sole designer and developer on this project. I will start by designing a logo for the client. Next, I designed the UI for their website. I created low-fi wireframes and final mockups of the UI of their website, as well as prototyped and developed the website.

Understand

Goals

Previously my client had been using social media to promote their work. However, this is not ideal because not everyone uses the same social media platforms. They needed a webpage to direct clients to so that everyone will be able to see their work. The overall goal was to be able to promote their business to everyone and display their work to the public.

The client needed a place on the internet to showcase their body of work to potential clients.

There also needed to be a place on the site that will allow people to contact them.

The website should have links to their social media.

Requirements and Constraints

  • A landing page with a hero image showcasing a slideshow of their work
  • A Gallery page with their photography
  • A place for clients to contact them
  • A logo for their website and products
  • A place for them to showcase their reel
  • Their site needs to be completed before Jan 1, 2020

Research

Branding and Logo Design

One of the requirements was to design a logo that the client could use for the website, business cards, or any other needs that the client might have. I experimented with some possible logo designs that might be used.

Design

Visual Exploration

lo-fi wireframes were created to explore possibilities for the layout of the site. Users need to be able to view their work and contact the photographer through the website. Another important detail was to add links to their social media sites so people could get to their businesses' Facebook and Instagram accounts.

Final UI

The Gallery page was one of the most important aspects of the site. I wanted the visual layout of the site to be pleasing to look at and allow the user to click on and view the images close up as well as on a grid.

I designed a contact form for the user to get in touch with the photographer and book appointments.

Solution

I was able to design a functional UI to give a photographer work and their business a home on the internet and allow them to grow past solely using social media platforms to showcase their work. This will allow the client to get more people's attention as well as have a more professional feel to their work and business.
