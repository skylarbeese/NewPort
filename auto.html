A Breif Overview

This project is to redesign the Automation and Electrical Engineering site for Utah Valley University's website. The Automation and electrical technology program needs to reach out to more potential students. Most people do not know about the program and if they do learn about major it is through word of mouth. The Automation and electrical technology program needs a better webpage to get more attention through there online presents. The overall goal is to get more people informed about the program through the UVU website. There needs to be more students signing up for the program.

Constraints

  • The project must be finished on Dec 16, 2021
  • the new website must work within the layout of UVUs content Management System
  • The new design must be consistent with the rest of the UVU website

Research and user interviews

The main concern of the stakeholder was to get more people aware of the program primarily the AAS degree because there were so few people applying to the program each year. The goal was to get more students to enter the program. Research needed to be conducted to see what people know about the program and who might be interested in it. We needed to understand what was most important to people regarding the job market.

"Attract more students into the program and give students availability for better lives."

Site Map

After the research on the users is done we must now take a look at the current webpage and begin the first stages of the redesign. The sitemap will help view the content and flow of the pages.

Several pages must be included in the final website layout. The first is a home page. This page will be the landing spot for the user and should contain important information about the program that we will want to highlight for the user. The next is an about page. This page should contain the mission statement of the program and other key information. The Degrees and programs page needs to have information about the courses. The students will also need a page where they can connect with an adviser and see the other faculty members and professors in the program.

Prototyping and User Testing

The final stages of the redesigned website was to create and test the prototype of the new layout. We tested 3 different areas of the site to make sure future students and potential students would be able to fine so of the more important areas of the site. The 5 test users were high school to college aged. They were as to do a handful of tasked on the website. Which included finding the advising and scholarships pages as well as finding more information about the major itself. Our testing concluded that we still had some redesigning and adjustments to make with some of the pages. Users where having a hard time finding the advising page. They where trying to find the advisers through the faculty and staff page. We fixed this problem by adding a link to the advising page on the faculty and staff page.
