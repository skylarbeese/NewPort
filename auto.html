Overview

This project is to redesign the Automation and Electrical Engineering site for Utah Valley University's website. The Automation and electrical technology program needs to reach out to more potential students. Most people do not know about the program and if they do learn about the major it is through word of mouth. The Automation and electrical technology program needs a better web page to get more attention through their online presents. The overall goal is to get more people informed about the program through the UVU website. There needs to be more students signing up for the program.

Constraints

  • The project must be finished on Dec 16, 2021
  • the new website must work within the layout of UVUs content Management System
  • The new design must be consistent with the rest of the UVU website

My Role

On this project, I will be working in a group with 5 others. The work will be divided and we will all be performing pieces of the research and design phase. I researched current students and people considering attending the University to get a better understanding of what aspects of the job market are most important to them. So we will know what things should be highlighted when redesigning the site and what might interest people in applying for the program. As well as asking about their current knowledge of the engineering program. Also seeing if they might be interested in enrolling in the program. I will also redesign 3 of the new pages that will be incorporated into the new layout.

Research and user interviews

The main concern of the department was to get more people aware of the program, primarily the AAS degree because there were so few people applying to the program each year. The goal was to get more students to enter the program. Research needed to be conducted to see what people know about the program and who might be interested in it. We needed to understand what was most important to people regarding the job market. So we would know how to promote the program on the website. We ended up surveying students on what time of jobs they may be interested in and how important things like salary and job security were to them. We ended up finding that security was a lot more important than their income. These are things that we could use to promote the program because the is a 100% job placement rate for grads.

"Attract more students into the program and give students availability for better lives."

Site Map

After the research on the users is done we started taking a look at the current webpage and began the first stages of the redesign. The sitemap will help view the content, flow of the pages and the information that is laid out on each page.

Several pages must be included in the final website layout. The first is a home page. This page will be the landing spot for the user and should contain important information about the program that we will want to highlight for the user. The next is an about page. This page should contain the mission statement of the program and other key information. The Degrees and programs page needs to have information about the courses. The students will also need a page where they can connect with an adviser and see the other faculty members and professors in the program.

Prototyping and User Testing

The final stages of the redesigned website is creating and testing the prototype of the new layout. We tested 3 different areas of the site to make sure future students would be able to find them.The 5 test users were high school to college aged. They were asked to complete a handful of tasks on the website. Which included finding the advising and scholarships pages. As well as finding more information about the major itself. Our testing concluded we still had some redesigning and adjustments to make with some of the pages. Users were having a hard time finding the advising page. They were trying to find the advisers through the faculty and staff page. We fixed this problem by adding a link to the advising page on the faculty and staff page.
